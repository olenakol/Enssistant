IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is the world’s most popular English language test with over 2.9 million tests taken each year. Taking IELTS can help you live, study and work around the world.

More than 10,000 organisations in over 140 countries worldwide accept IELTS for education, employment and migration. IELTS is the only test accepted for immigration purposes by all countries that require one.

The IELTS test is designed to reflect how you will use English in your new life abroad, and, within about three hours, it will assess all of your English skills: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking.

Uniquely, IELTS tests your English conversation skills through an interactive face-to-face speaking test with a trained, certified examiner.

IELTS has been developed by some of the world’s leading experts in English language assessment, so you can be sure that it is a fair test.

IELTS and the Common European Framework (CEFR)

IELTS is also recognised by the Council of Europe. The Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) provides a basis for the mutual recognition of language qualifications, thus facilitating educational and occupational mobility.

IELTS scores CEFR level
3.5–4.0 A2
4.5–5.0 B1
5.5–6.5 B2
7.0–7.5 C1
8.0–9.0 C2
